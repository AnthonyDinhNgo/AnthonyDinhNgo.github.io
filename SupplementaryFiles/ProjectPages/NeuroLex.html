Computational Neuroscience Research

2018 - Present


My Role

Involved in computational undergraduate research on Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia


Exploratory Data Analysis

I began my work with NeuroLex by performing exploratory data analysis on Alzheimer's Disease and Dementia Data from the Framingham Heart Study, primarily looking at linguistic and vocal features. The goal was to find possible connections between the way someone speaks and their cognitive status. I found two significant patterns, one of which was included in an academic paper later on. I spent around five months on data exploration through Python and Pandas



Data Visualization

My second role with NeuroLex Labs involved creating concise and efficient data visualization that would effectively communicate the trends I found during my exploratory analysis.



Co-Author a Paper

I concluded my first year with NeuroLex Labs by becoming a contributing author on a paper surrounding vocal indicators of degenerative neurological diseases. I provided a thorough explanation of my findings from my exploratory analysis, a discussion on why we used certain python toolkits (like SpaCy and NLTK), and a few of my data visualizations
